Evaluation of a function related group (FRG) payment model for inpatient medical rehabilitation.

Sutton JP, DeJong G, Wilkerson MA.

AHSR FHSR Annu Meet Abstr Book. 1995; 12: 100-1.

National Rehabilitation Hospital Research Center, Washington, DC 20010, USA.

PROBLEMS AND OBJECTIVES. This NIDRR-funded study examines the relationship between a Medicare case-mix index, developed using a function-based classification model (FIM-FRGs) and the average costs per case for rehabilitation facilities. The results of this study are currently being used to simulate the fiscal impact of various payment alternatives on inpatient medical rehabilitation providers. DATA AND METHODS. A case-mix index, with relative weights derived from the average length of stay for each grouping, was developed for a sample of rehabilitation hospitals and units which submitted 1990 patient level data on admission functioning and charges to the Uniform Data System for Medical Rehabilitation (UDS-MR). Hospital cost functions (N=47) were designed to test the validity of this case-mix index and to examine the magnitude of adjusters for factors, such as teaching status, which are generally associated with higher costs. RESULTS AND CONCLUSIONS. Although the relationship between the case-mix index and average costs was found to be approximately proportional, the regression equation explained only 21% of the variation in costs. Hospitals costs were also found to be positively associated with bed size and a local wage index. Teaching status was not found to be a significant predictor of costs. IMPLICATIONS FOR AUDIENCE. Preliminarily, it appears that the FIM-FRG derived case-mix index is a valid predictor of expected costs among rehabilitation providers. Nevertheless, further investigation is necessary to determine the feasibility of a PPS for rehabilitation providers. A prospective payment system assumes that although facilities experience net losses for some cases and net gain for other cases, on average, over all cases, reimbursement will adequately cover costs. Because many facilities had few cases in certain high cost function related groups, it will be necessary to examine the fiscal impact of alternative specifications of the model as well as alternative outlier policies in order to reduce the risk of systematic losses which may result from random fluctuations in case mix.
