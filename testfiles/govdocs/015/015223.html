Nature and pattern of psychotropic drug use in hospital.

Udomprasertgul V; International Society of Technology Assessment in Health Care. Meeting.

Abstr Int Soc Technol Assess Health Care Meet. 1992; 5.

Institute of Health Research, Chulalongkorn University, Phya-Thai Road, Bangkok, Thailand.

The use, misuse and abuse of certain psychotropic substances may create public health and social problems. It is important that any health authority follow and survey doctors' prescribing habits. Drug consumption studies can also be used to measure the efficacy of health education of the public as well as to measure the influence of educational activities aimed at physicians. In principle, it is in the interest of all persons planning or controlling the public health system in the country. This study was conducted in Surin province of the northwestern region. Population samples were recruited from four hospitals. The study sample consisted of all out-patients from the four sample hospitals that received at least one type of psychotropic drug for treatment of their illness for five months. The patients were interviewed on the day of their visit to the hospital. 54,248 patients were registered at the provincial hospital and 17,952, 11,816 and 6,241 cases for the three small community hospitals, respectively. 9.1% of those attending the provincial hospital received a prescription containing a psychotropic drug in comparison to 6.1%, 5.6% and 6.3% of those attending the community hospitals. The average age of all cases recruited were between 40-45 years. Insomnia was commonly reported in conjunction with headache (75-82%) or dyspnoea (56-73%). It was interesting to observe that 42-65% of patients reported being uninformed of their diagnosis after examination. However, the three most common diseases were gastritis, neurosis and heart disease or hypertension. The majority, 69-80%, received only one psychotropic drug. It should be noted that 2-7% received three to five different psychotropic drugs. Diazepam was the most frequent psychotropic agent which was prescribed for 71-90% of the cases. This study suggests that patients had little chance of knowing the appropriate use of drugs. Psychotropic drugs are controlled under physician prescription. The present study demonstrates that the hospital treatment could unintentionally lead to probably incorrect use in communities.
