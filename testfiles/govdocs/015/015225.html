Anxiety risk-state and depression in 540 patients with risk practices.

Rossi L, Figueroa A, Garcia L, Chavez Peon F, Sepulveda J; International Conference on AIDS.

Int Conf AIDS. 1991 Jun 16-21; 7: 279 (abstract no. W.B.2389).

CONASIDA, Mexico City

OBJECTIVE: To determine the anxiety risk-state and depression degree in patients that attend at AIDS Information Center to get the Anti-VIH test for the first time. METHOD: Before the Anti-VIH test, the State-trait Anxiety Inventory "Self Evaluation Questionnaire" (IDARE), consisting of two self evaluation scales, with 20 reactives each one, one measuring general anxiety (trait) and the other anxiety when applying the test (state), were used. The Zung depression self-measuring scale (EAMD) is constituted of 20 reactives that measure severe depression, small relevant depressive traits and normal depression to 540 patients with risk practices that attended voluntarily to get the anti-VIH test. 50% were men, 66.97% younger than 29 years, 37.03 older than 30. The women's group, 66.67% younger than 29, 33.33% older than 30. The results obtained from the two instruments were compared, to determine the prevalence of anxiety and/or depression. RESULTS: State-anxiety in men and women, presented an X=55.03, S=12.25 and a significance value of 0.27 and 0.209 respectively, that correspond to a normal anxiety state, trait anxiety presented an X=52.55, - S=13.22 and a significance value F 0.378 and 0.487 in each group (men and women). In relation to the norm, state anxiety predominates, which signifies that in the moment of applying the test there is a stressful situation. Depression presented an X=43.42, S=13.64 and a significance value F 0.175 (men) and 0.611 (women) that means small relevant depressive traits, but indicates latent depression. CONCLUSION: Although no significant statistical differences are found, we can conclude that the reason why state anxiety is greater than trait anxiety and depression is that: a) Anxiety as symptom precedes depression. b) Patients go over a working-through process before getting the Anti-HIV test; this diminishes trait anxiety but increases state anxiety for major conscience of the test. c) Social rejection fantasies increase before the possibility of a positive serological result. All this information is useful for the subsequent psychological conduction of the patient.
