QUANTITATIVE PRECIPITATION FORECAST DISCUSSION
NWS HYDROMETEOROLOGICAL PREDICTION CENTER CAMP SPRINGS MD
320 AM EST MON FEB 02 2009


PRELIM DAY 1...DAY 2 AND DAY 3 QPF DISCUSSION
VALID FEB 02/1200 UTC THRU FEB 05/1200 UTC
REFERENCE AWIPS GRAPHICS UNDER...PRECIP ACCUM - 24HR

DAY 1...

...MID ATLC STATES SSWWD INTO THE GULF CST REGION...
LATEST MDL RUNS ARE IN RATHER GOOD AGREEMENT IN AMPLIFYING THE H5
TROF THRU THE GTLKS..OH VLY AND DOWN INTO THE SERN STATES DURG
THIS PD..WITH NRN STREAM S/WV ENERGY DIGGING SSEWD WHILE SRN
STREAM ENERGY MOVS EWD ACRS THE GULF OF MEX AND THEN LIFTS NEWD
INTO THE SWRN ATLC.  THE CDFNT ASSOCTD WITH THE NRN STREAM ENERGY
WL BE ONE FOCUS FOR PCPN..BUT WITH THE MUCH DEEPER MSTR RMNG WELL
S WITH THE SRN BRANCH ENERGY..XPC PCPN TOTALS ALG THE FNTL BNDRY
TO BE ON THE LGT SIDE..IN GENL AGREEMENT WITH THE MDL QPF SOLNS.
HVIER RNFL WL BE WITH THE SRN STREAM..WITH RECENT RADAR ALREADY
SHOWING INCREASING RNFL COVERAGE ACRS THE TN VLY AND CNTRL/WRN
GULF CST STATES AND SATL IMAGERY SHOWING COOLING TOPS..ESP ALG THE
IMMEDIATE GULF CST AND INTO THE NRN GULF OF MEX.  AS THIS SRN
STREAM S/WV ENERGY MOVS EWD THRU THE NRN GULF MON AND THEN A LTL
MORE NEWD MON NGT..XPC THE ACCOMPANYING STRENGTHENING GULF INFLO
AND INCREASING PWS TO SUPPORT EXPANDING RAINS/CNVCTN FM THE CNTRL
GULF CST AREA EWD INTO MUCH OF THE FL PENIN.  THE GFS LOOKS TO BE
SUFFERING FM FEEDBACK PROBS A BIT THIS REGION..SO ITS QPF MAY BE A
LTL TOO HIGH..BUT IN GENL THE IDEA OF MDT TO LCLY HVY RNFL
SPREADING ESP INTO THE CNTRL FL PENIN ALG H5 S/WV TRACK AND VCNTY
OF SFC WV SEEMS REASONABLE.  THUS..XPC A RELATIVE RNFL ACRS MSTLY
THE CNTRL FL PENIN..WITH SOME ISOLD GTR THAN 1 INCH AMTS LIKELY.
SOME FAVORABLE INFLO INTO THE WRN FL PNHNDL AND SRN AL SHLD ALSO
SUPPORT ANOTHER RNFL MAX THERE..GENLY IN LN WITH MDL SOLNS.


STRENGTHENING WRN U.S. RDG/BACKING OF THE FLO OVR THE NERN PAC WL
SHUNT THE HIER MSTR NWD INTO B.C. THIS PD..LEAVING ONLY SOME VRY
LGT RAINS PTNS OF THE EXTRM NWRN PARTS OF WA AND THE NRN WA
CASCADES.  AND FINALLY..WIDESPREAD BUT GENLY LGT SNOWS WL COVER
MUCH OF THE GTLKS REGION..UNDER PRETTY IMPRESSIVE AND COLD H5
TROF.

DAYS 2 AND 3...

...CONCERNING THE AMPLIFYING ERN NOAM LONGWV TROF...

MOST OF THE MOISTURE ASSOCIATED WITH THIS SYSTEM WILL HAVE BEEN
SHED INTO THE ATLANTIC BY DAY 2...BUT THE MAJORITY OF SOLUTIONS
HAVE WRAP-AROUND PRECIP SWINGING THROUGH COASTAL NEW ENGLAND.
ENSEMBLE MEANS ARE HIGHLIGHTING THIS EVEN MORE THAN THE PREFERRED
NAM/GFS OPERATIONALS...SO HAVE USED NAM/GFS FOR GENERAL TIMING AND
PLACEMENT...BUT DID INCREASED AMOUNTS TO SHOW A QUARTER TO HALF
INCH ALONG THE NEW ENGLAND COAST FALLING MOSTLY DURING D2.
FARTHER UPSTREAM...ERRATIC VORT ENERGY FLOWING THROUGH THE
LONGWAVE TROUGH CONTINUES TO BE A CHALLENGE FOR THE MODELS...SO
HAVE KEPT A BROADBRUSH OF LIGHT QPF ACROSS THE OHIO VALLEY IN THE
UNSTABLE AIRMASS UNDERNEATH THE TROUGH ON DAY 2...CLEARING OUT
FROM W TO E ON DAY 3.

...GREAT LAKES...

PERSISTENT NORTH WINDS ON THE BACK SIDE OF THE ERN TROUGH WILL
INDUCE LAKE-EFFECT SNOWS TO ALL THE LAKES ON D2...GRADUALLY WANING
ON DAY 3.  THE GREATEST FETCH WILL BE DOWN THE N-S PATH OF LAKE
MICHIGAN...SO HIGHEST QPF HAS BEEN PLACED AT THE SOUTH END OF THIS
LAKE.  A QUARTER INCH OF LIQUID-EQUIVALENT COULD FALL HERE.

...CNTRL CA COAST...

CLOSED UPR LOW WILL BREAK FROM THE NORTHERN STREAM IN THE ERN PAC
AND MEANDER SE LATE IN DAY 3.  SIGNIFICANT MODEL DIFFERENCES...BUT
THE GFS HAS BEEN USED FOR TIMING WHICH BRINGS PRECIP TO THE
CENTRAL CA COAST BY THE LAST 6 HRS OF D3.  HAVE PLACED A
TENTH..LOCAL QUARTER...TO THE COASTAL HIGHER TERRAIN.

TERRY/SOWKO
