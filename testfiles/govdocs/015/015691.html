Do Bugs Need Drugs? Website.

BLONDEL-HILL E, FRYTERS S, MITCHELL S, TOMNEY M, WILSON D, CARSON M; Interscience Conference on Antimicrobial Agents and Chemotherapy (41st : 2001 : Chicago, Ill.).

Abstr Intersci Conf Antimicrob Agents Chemother Intersci Conf Antimicrob Agents Chemother. 2001 Dec 16-19; 41: abstract no. W-277.

University of Alberta, Edmonton, AB, Canada

Background. Antibiotic resistance is a community health issue related to overuse of antibiotics, especially for viral respiratory tract infections (RTIs). Incognizance of treatment guidelines among physicians and the public has contributed to this problem. As the Internet is increasingly used as a resource for health information, www.dobugsneeddrugs.org was developed to expand an educational campaign in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. Methods. The website, posted March 2, 2001, has six sections: General Public, Parents, Teachers, Kids, Healthcare Professionals and Occupational Health. Topics include: key messages in English and other languages, educational video, care guide for children with RTIs, handwashing instructions, teacher resources, games, link to Clinical Practice Guidelines for RTIs, Bugs and Drugs Antimicrobial Pocket Reference 2001, adult tutorial, links to relevant websites and Tip of the Week for the public and for healthcare professionals which can be e-mailed automatically to subscribers. Results. The site had 56,000 hits in the first six weeks of posting despite having minimal on-line promotion. The most often requested directory was Kids (34%) followed by Healthcare Professional (22%) and Teacher (20%). Ear ache, sinus infection and handwashing were the most common search queries used to reach the site via search engines. Twelve requests for the Tip of the Week for the public had been received and none for the healthcare professionals tips. Eight requests for materials had been received via the website. Conclusions. These observations suggest that the local educational campaign has stimulated interest in both the community and healthcare professional aspects of the website. Ongoing monitoring will indicate changes in use and scope of the audience as the site is promoted and expanded.
