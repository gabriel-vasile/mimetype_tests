Notes:

    Propane Stocks Return to Normal
  • With production being relatively flat, and demand being highly seasonal, propane stocks play an important role over the winter months.
  • Propane stocks satisfy about 20 percent of demand during the peak months of December, January and February. As a result, prices are sensitive to winter stock levels.
  • Last year, stocks were depleted over the cold winter of 1995/96 and ended September 1996 at their third lowest level for start of the winter heating season. Hopes of a late fall build were dashed with cold weather and late crop-drying needs that boosted demand in the Midwest in October.
  • Fortunately, warm weather throughout the country during the peak winter months helped ease stock pressures.
  • Weak demand and strong production over the first 7 months of 1997 have brought stocks back above average.
    • The net result is that stocks ended August at 57.3 million barrels, about 2 percent over the five year average, and 8 million barrels above August 1996.
    • Canadian stocks are also high this year, boding well for import potential.
  • Healthy stock levels in both the U.S. and Canada, along with continued high production, should easily accommodate increased demand with seasonal price increases, given stable crude prices.
