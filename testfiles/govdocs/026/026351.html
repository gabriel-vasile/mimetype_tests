BIOLOGY: The California red scale (CRS) is a biparental, ovoviviparous species, that may infest all the above-ground parts of host plants (Ferris, 1938a; Bartlett, 1978). Crawlers of CRS responded to various solutions behind artificial membranes by secreting wax threads that form the scale cover. This response was used to evaluate gustatory stimuli responsible for crawler settling (Walker & Bendar, 1986). The sex pheromne of CRS has been identified and synthesized (Roelofs et al., 1978). Millar & Hare (1993) isolated and identified a kairomone from the scale cover, which functions as an oviposition stimulant.
