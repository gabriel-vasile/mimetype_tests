" Tree and Shrub Installation Specification -General Planting Operation                                                                                                                                                                                                 
Plants shall be either Balled and Burlapped, Bare Root, or Containerized. Plants shall be dug and handled with root system intact and as a complete solid root ball. The size (diameter and depth) shall be sufficient to encompass the fibrous root feeding system and 
as specified by A.A.N Standards. Root balls shall be adequately protected at all times from sun and/or drying winds. Plant material shall be bound with wire or rope, and tarp to minimize any damage in transit. Initial Watering shall be performed as part of plant i
nstallation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            
" Tree and Shrub Installation Specification - Excavation of Planting Area                                                                                                                                                                                               
All planting pits shall have the sides and bottom scarified to ensure proper soil interaction with plant material's root ball. If extraneous material; such as stones, earth clods, sticks, minor construction debris, etc., is encountered when excavating, it shall be
 removed by contractor. A plant pit shall be excavated for each plant provided as follows:                                                                                                                                                                              
Balled and Burlapped: Excavation shall be a minimum of six (6) inches wider than the plant root ball      diameter.                                                                                                                                                     
Containerized Plants: Excavation for container plants shall be twice as wide as the plant  container.                                                                                                                                                                   
Bare Root Plants:  Excavations shall be at least six (6) inches wider than the root spread and deep    enough to allow setting of roots or a cone of compacted fill.                                                                                                    
General Planting Operation for each plant shall as stated in the following guidelines:                                                                                                                                                                                  
Balled and Burlapped: Plant material shall be set plumb and held in position until the  planting soil has been tamped solidly around the ball and roots. Ropes or strings shall be cut from the top  of the ball after plant has been set.  Burlap or cloth wrapping sha
ll remain intact around root balls.  Top wrapping top, one-third (1/3), shall be folded back or removed. Plants shall be set approximately one-eighth (1/8) the depth of the root ball higher than the relationship to finished grade as they bore to the ground from wh
ich they were dug. A soil saucer shall be formed around trees and shrubs. Planting pits and planting beds shall be mulched with, a minimum layer of two (2) inches and a maximum layer of three (3) inches, of shredded hardwood mulch. Upon completion, all tags, label
s, strings, surveying tape, etc. shall be removed from all plants.                                                                                                                                                                                                      
Containerized Plants:  Excavation for container plants shall be twice as wide as the plant container.  Planting pits and planting beds shall be mulched with, a minimum layer of two (2)  inches and a maximum layer of three (3) inches, of shredded hardwood mulch. Up
on  completion, trees shall be staked, all tags, labels, strings, surveying tape, etc. shall  be removed from all plants. Containers shall be completely removed around the roots  before planting. A  razor knife or equal shall be used to cut an incision a minimum o
f  one-inch (1") into  the root ball from top to bottom.  A minimum of four (4) vertical  incisions shall be  made per plant before planting.                                                                                                                           
Bare Root Plants:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
Excavations shall be at least six (6) inches wider than the root spread and deep enough to allow setting of roots or a cone of  compacted fill. Planting pits and planting beds shall be mulched with, a minimum layer of two (2) inches and a  maximum layer of three (
3) inches, of shredded hardwood mulch. Upon completion, all tags, labels, strings, surveying tape, etc. shall be removed from all plants.                                                                                                                               
" Tree and Shrub Installation Specification - Planting Mix Backfill                                                                                                                                                                                                     
Contractor shall back-fill all planting pit areas with a mixture of 90% existing excavated topsoil and 10% LEAFGRO (organic matter). Planting mix shall be proportioned and thoroughly mixed prior to any back filling of planting pits.
