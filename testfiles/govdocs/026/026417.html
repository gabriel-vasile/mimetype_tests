DDR1 (CD167a)
Author: Wolfgang Vogel
Reviewer: Hans-Joerg Buehring
PROW 1:41-46(2000)

OVERVIEW
Summary Sentence
  • DDR1 (CD167a) is a receptor tyrosine kinase (RTK) widely expressed in normal and transformed epithelial cells and is activated by various types of collagen
 Abstract
  • DDR1 belongs to a subfamily of tyrosine kinase receptors with a homology region to the Dictyostelium discoideum protein discoidin I in their extracellular domain. So far, two members have been identified, discoidin domain receptor 1 and 2 (DDR1 and DDR2). During the cell aggregation of Dictyostelium, discoidin I is secreted, functions as a lectin and is thought to be important in maintenance of morphology, cytoskeletal organization, and the ability to align with other cells during aggregation. The discoidin domain in the extracellular regions of both, DDR1 and DDR2, are thought to play a role in cell-cell contact and in cell adhesion signaling pathways
  • Various types of collagen have been identified as the cognate ligands for both DDRs. Whereas DDR1 autophosphorylation is achieved by all collagens so far tested (type I to type VI), DDR2 is only activated by fibrillar collagens, in particular by collagen type I and type III. In contrast to most other tyrosine kinase receptors, DDR activation follows very slow kinetics, requiring up to 18 hours to reach maximal kinase activity. The presence of the discoidin domain in DDR is thought to be essential for collagen binding. DDR1 activation takes place in the absence of a functional beta1 integrin receptor for collagen
  • cDNAs coding for DDR1 have been cloned from several human carcinoma cells, and from placenta and keratinocytes. Genes homologous to human DDR1 have been identified in mouse, rat and Caenorhabditis elegans. In situ studies and Northern-blot analysis showed that expression of human DDR1 is restricted to epithelial cells, particularly in the kidney, lung, gastrointestinal tract, and brain. In addition, DDR1 is significantly over-expressed in several human tumors from breast, ovarian, esophageal, and pediatric brain
  • Human DDR1 is located on chromosome 6p21.3 in proximity to several HLA class I genes. Mouse DDR1 is located on chromosome 17C. The human gene is composed of 17 exons; the extracellular domain is encoded by 8 exons, three of which encode the discoidin domain. One exon encodes the transmembrane region, three the juxtamembrane domain, and five the catalytic domain. Compared to other RTK, the juxtamembrane region of DDR1 is significantly longer (176 amino acids). DDR1 is partially processed into a 63 kDa membrane-anchored beta-subunit and a 54 kDa soluble, extracellular domain-containing alpha-subunit, by a yet unidentified protease. So far, three isoforms of DDR1 generated by alternative splicing, were identified: DDR1a, DDR1b and DDR1c. The longest transcript codes for the c-isoform and translates to a protein with 919 amino acids. Compared to the c-isoform, the b-isoform lacks 6 amino acids inserted between exon 13 and 14. The a-isoform lacks an additional 37 amino acids in the juxtamembrane region as a result of alternative splicing of exon 10 to exon 12. DDR1b protein is the predominant isoform expressed during embryogenesis, whereas the a-isoform is upregulated in certain human mammary carcinoma cell lines. The relative expression ratio and the post-translational modification of the a- and b-isoform seem to underlie complex regulatory mechanisms. Overexpression of DDR1a and DDR1b in human embryonic kidney fibroblast 293 cells revealed a differential glycosylation pattern of the two isoforms. The alternatively spliced 37 amino acids insert in DDR1b contains the motif LLXNPXY, which can associate with the phosphotyrosine-binding domain of the Shc adaptor protein upon collagen-induced tyrosine phosphorylation. The fibroblast-growth factor receptor substrate 2 (FRS2) binds to a chimeric molecule containing the juxtamembrane region of DDR1a

SYSTEM VIEW
Disease Relevance / Function in Vivo
  • To date, no human genetic disease is known that is linked to DDR1
  • DDR1 knockout mice are smaller in size, particular females
  • Blastocyst implantation is perturbed in the majority of knockout females
  • Knockout females that reproduce fail to lactate due to malformation of the mammary gland
 Cellular Function
  • DDR1 is activated by fibrillar collagens (type I, II, III and V) as well as basement membrane collagen (IV and VIII)
  • Upon stimulation with collagen, the tyrosine kinase is activated
  • Activation follows slow kinetics, maximal activation is seen 2 - 18 h after stimulation
  • In the alternatively spliced insert of DDR1b, the tyrosine in the sequence LLXNPXY becomes phosphorylated and binds to the PTB domain of the ShcA adaptor protein
  • The juxtamembrane region of DDR1a binds to the adaptor FRS2
  • No further downstream target has been identified

Post-transcriptional Modification
  • DDR1 shows alternative splicing in the juxtamembrane region and the kinase domain
  • The longest transcript is DDR1c (NM_013994)
  • Deletion of 18 bp in the kinase region results in the DDR1b isoform (NM_001954). A cryptic splice acceptor site has been identified 5' of exon 14
  • Deletion of exon 11 (111bp) in the juxtamembrane region of DDR1b results in DDR1a (NM_013993). A possible mechanism for production of this splice variant may be the presence of an inverted repeat at the 3' end of the exon/intron boundary which allows the formation of a hairpin structure
  • Message stability has not been studied
 Transcript Annotation
  • A single transcript of approximately 4.4 kb is detected by Northern hybridization

  • Kinase domain has high homology to mammalian TrkA, B and C, and to the marine sponge tyrosine kinase receptor GCTK (from Geodia cydonium)

 Protein Physical Properties
  • DDR1a and DDR1b are differentially glycosylated
  • Several tyrosines become phosphorylated upon receptor activation
  • Tyrosine 513 of DDR1b (part of the motif LLXNPXY) has been mapped as phosphorylation site
  • About 10% of DDR1 is proteolytically processed yielding a 52 kDa soluble protein (alpha-subunit) and a 62 kDa transmembrane protein (beta-subunit)
  • The beta-subunit remains in the membrane, the alpha-subunit can be purified from the supernatant
  • The Furin recognition site RFRR (amino acid 304-307) may be utilized for cleavage (shedding) of DDR1
