QUANTITATIVE PRECIPITATION FORECAST

 
 FXUS04 KWBC 051831
 QPFPFD
  
 QUANTITATIVE PRECIPITATION FORECAST DISCUSSION
 NWS HYDROMETEOROLOGICAL PREDICTION CENTER CAMP SPRINGS MD
 130 PM EST THU FEB 05 2009
  
  
 PRELIM DAY 1 QPF DISCUSSION
 VALID FEB 06/0000 UTC THRU FEB 07/0000 UTC
 REFERENCE AWIPS GRAPHICS UNDER...PRECIP ACCUM - 24HR
  
  
 DAY 1...
 
 ...WRN U.S...
 
 THE MEAN TROF WL PERSIST ALONG THE WEST COAST DAY 1...WITH THE
 EMPHASIS FOR A CLOSED LOW IN THE MEAN OFF THE SRN CA COAST. 
 OVERALL..MODEL SOLNS DAY 1 ARE IN GOOD AGRMNT.  ALL MODELS SHOW
 THE INITIAL CLOSED LOW OFF THE CNTRL TO SRN CA COAST BEING KICKED
 INLAND BY ADDITIONAL HT FALLS DIVING SWD OFF THE CA COAST AND INTO
 THE MEAN CLOSED LOW PSN.  THE INITIAL INLAND MOVG CLOSED LOW AND
 THE REFORMATION OF THE CLOSED LOW TONIGHT INTO FRI WL KEEP
 SUSTAINED SLY TO SWLY ONSHORE FLOW INTO A LARGE PORTION OF CA. 
 THIS WL SUPPORT MDT TO HVY PCPN AMTS IN THE FAVORED UPSLOPE
 REGIONS FROM FAR SWRN OR/NWRN CA...SWD ALONG THE COAST RANGE OF
 CNTRL TO SRN CA...ACRS THE TRANSVERSE RANGE AND  INTO THE SRN
 SIERRA.  FOR QPF DETAILS..THE 12 KM NAM AND SREF MEAN WERE
 FOLLOWED MORE CLOSELY.
 
 LARGE SCALE LIFT AND RH WL INCREASE ACRS THE GTBASIN WITH THE
 INLAND MOVEMENT OF THE INITIAL CLOSED LOW OFF THE CA COAST.  PCPN
 AMTS WL BE LIGHTER THAN UPSTREAM ALONG THE CA COAST GIVEN LOWER PW
 VALUES AND THE WEAKENING TREND OF THE CLOSED LOW.  MODELS ARE
 SHOWING A LOT OF SPREAD WITH QPF TO THE EAST OF THE SRN SIERRA
 INTO FAR ERN CA AND SRN NV.  A FEW OF THE SREF MEMBERS SHOW
 .25-.50"+ AMTS HERE...WHILE OTHERS HAVE CONSIDERABLY LESS IN THE
 .10"+RANGE.  ATTM...WE TRENDED AWAY FROM THE HEAVIER TOTALS AS
 THEY SEEMED OVERDONE...DEPICTING AMTS CLOSER TO THE OPERATIONAL
 ECMWF.  
 
 WHILE THE CRUX OF THE NERN PAC HT FALLS ARE EXPECTED TO DIVE SWD
 OFF THE WEST COAST AND INTO THE MEAN CLOSED LOW PSN OFF THE SRN CA
 COAST...SOME HT FALLS WL MOVE INTO THE PAC NW AND TOWARD THE NRN
 ROCKIES FRI. THIS WL SUPPORT LIGHT TO MDT PCPN AMTS IN THE
 .10-.25"+ RANGE ACRS THE PAC NW INTO THE NRN ROCKIES
 
 COASTAL SE FL...
 
 THE LOW LEVEL FLOW IS EXPECTED TO VEER TO A MORE ELY DIRECTION
 ACRS S FL AS THE SFC HIGH OVR THE SOUTHEAST MOVES OFFSHORE.  THIS
 SHOULD SUPPORT AN INCREASE IN SHOWER ACTIVITY LATE TONIGHT INTO
 FRI OVR THE COASTAL SECTIONS OF SE FL.  DAY 1 QPF DID NOT DEVIATE
 FROM THE OPERATIONAL AND SREF CONSENSUS OF AREAL AVG LIGHT PCPN
 AMTS IN THESE SHOWERS...ALTHOUGH ISOLATED HEAVY TOTALS ARE
 POSSIBLE WITH ELY FLOW RAINBANDS. 
 
 ORAVEC
