Justin Clarity is a participant in the NESLS program and is being mentored by Jess Gehin of the Reactor Analysis (RA) group. Nuclear Engineering degree with an expected graduation date in December 2006.  Also, Justin plans to pursue an advanced education in Nuclear Engineering.  When asked what interests led him into the field of nuclear engineering Justin replied, “It combines almost every type of science and engineering skill, which make is challenging. Plus nuclear technology is something the U.S. is not likely to outsource to another country soon.”  He also stated that he enjoyed taking a class with Dr. Gehin at UT and asked Dr. Gehin if there was anything he could do in his group, hence the NESLS internship. Justin’s summer research project involves coding benchmark between KENO-Va and PARCS performed on a 2x2 array of BWR fuel assemblies.

Justin’s hobbies include playing racquetball and reading.

Jess Gehin expands on Justin’s summer research project: Justin is working on a project to understand the reactor physics modeling needs for  boiling water reactor fuel assemblies in support of a project for Nuclear Regulatory Commission.  In this project Justin will be developing a high-fidelity, three-dimensional model that can be used for evaluation of the standard calculational methods, which generally rely on two-dimensional models. The results of comparisons of the 3-D and 2-D methods will provide a basis for either confirming that the 2-D methods are acceptable or indicating the need for improvements of the calculational methods.  In addition to providing the model that we need at ORNL, Justin will gain experience in the use of the SCALE code system, unix-based workstation clusters, and BWR reactor physics.
