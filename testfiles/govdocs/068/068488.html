 OB:  Biological control of primary pests of vegetable crops, using native and
 introduced natural enemies. 
 AP:  Study the ecological, bionomical, taxonomical, and faunal characteristics
 of beneficial and pest species of vegetable crops. Develop control strategies
 based on these factors, with emphasis on conservation and augmentation,
 foreign exploration, and integrated control. 
 PR:  6301 TO 8806 
      During the 25-year life of the project, studies were conducted on
 arthropod pests associated with the principal vegetable crops grown in
 southern California, with emphasis on biological control of the potato
 tuberworm on potato, tomato pinworm on tomato, two-spotted spider mite on
 strawberry, and Amorbia cuneana and omnivorous looper on avocado. Biological
 control of the latter three pests was highly successful, and is used commonly
 by strawberry and avocado growers statewide. Integrated control programs were
 developed successfully for tomato and cole crops, using mass releases of
 Trichogramma and applications of Bacillus thuringiensis for "key" pest
 species. These results were incorporated in IPM manuals for the two crops. The
 biologies of most exotic and indigenous hymenopterous parasites of the potato
 tuberworm and tomato pinworm were studied. Additionally, biosystematic studies
 of Trichogramma, parasitizing eggs of lepidopterous pests, were conducted.
 Altogether, 126 publications were published on results obtained from the
 project research.
