Participants and Facilities

Facilities utilized by, and organizations involved with, the program include:
    NASA Ames Research Center - within the program, Ames Intelligent Mechanisms Group focuses on applying virtual reality and telepresence control technology to robotic systems
      Intelligent Mechanisms Laboratory

    NASA Johnson Space Center - the JSC Automation, Robotics and Simulation Division supports the development of robotic systems for on-orbit applications, including systems for the International Space Station
      Robotic Systems Evaluation Laboratory
      Integrated Graphics Operations and Analysis Laboratory
      Systems Engineering Laboratory
      Telepresence/Virtual Reality Laboratory
      Intelligent Systems Laboratory
      Robotic Maintenance for Space Station Facility
      Manipulator Development Facility

    Jet Propulsion Laboratory - the JPL Robotics Section provides planetary rover technology and fundamental robotics technology development for the program, in addition to the JPL lead center responsibilities for the program
      Surface Inspection Robotics Laboratory
      Mobile Robotics Laboratory
      Supervised Telerobotics Laboratory

    Carnegie-Mellon University - the CMU participation in the program is targeted at the development of prototype lunar rover technologies, as well as the commercialization of program-developed robotics technologies
      Robotics Institute
      Field Robotics Center
      Learning Robot Laboratory
      Software Engineering Institute
      Robotics Engineering Consortium
      Vision and Autonomous Systems Center

    Massachusetts Institute of Technology - MIT provides subsumption-architecture robotics, haptic interface systems, and task control technology for the program
      Field and Space Robotics Laboratory
      Micro-Robotics Laboratory
      Mobile Robotics Laboratory
      Haptic Interfaces Laboratory
      Vision and Touch Guided Manipulation Laboratory

    Stanford University - for the program, the Stanford Aerospace Robotics Lab develops high-level control technology for multiple interacting robots and free-floating robotic systems
      Aerospace Robotics Laboratory

    University of Maryland - the program supports UMd Space Systems Lab for the development of free-flying robotic systems, and space operations research in interacting human-robotic systems
      UMd Neutral Buoyancy Research Facility
      UMd Space Systems Laboratory

    University of Texas at Austin - UT-A participation in the program is centered on the development of fault-tolerant mechanisms and control technology
      Robotics Research Laboratory
