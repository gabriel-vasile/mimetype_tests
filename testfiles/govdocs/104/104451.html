RESULTS NOT DEMONSTRATED PROGRAMS

WHAT DOES THE RESULTS NOT DEMONSTRATED RATING MEAN?

The program rating indicates how well a program is performing, so the public can see how effectively tax dollars are being spent.

A rating of Results Not Demonstrated (RND) indicates that a program has not been able to develop acceptable performance goals or collect data to determine whether it is performing. Results Not Demonstrated programs are categorized as Not Performing on ExpectMore.gov.

Based on our most recent assessments, 17% of Federal programs are Results Not Demonstrated.

More information on how we assess and rate programs is available here. Funding information for each program can be found with the President's Budget.
