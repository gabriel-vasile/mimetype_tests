Charles J. Glinka


Charles Glinka is a condensed matter physicist at the Center for Neutron Research at the National Institute for Standards and Technology in Gaithersburg, MD. He oversees the Research Facility Operations group, and is the project director for the NIST/NSF Center for High Resolution Neutron Scattering (CHRNS) at the NCNR.

Career History/Education:

  • Ph.D., Solid State Physics, U. of Maryland, College Park, MD (1975)
  • (2004 – present) Research Facility Operations Group Leader, NIST Center for Neutron Research
  • 1994 – 2003 Macromolecular and Microstructure Science Team Leader, NIST Center for Neutron Research
  • 1978 – 1994 Research Physicist, NIST Center for Neutron Research
  • 1977 - 1978 Research Associate, University of Maryland
  • 1975 - 1977 National Research Council Postdoctoral Fellow, National Bureau of Standards

Research Interests:

Applications of small angle neutron scattering to materials science including the characterization of microporous materials and physical and chemical phenomena occurring in microporous media, the behavior of complex fluids under shear, and mechanisms for materials synthesis through molecular self-assembly; neutron optics; neutron scattering instrumentation and techniques.
