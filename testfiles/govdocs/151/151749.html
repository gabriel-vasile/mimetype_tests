D
 Hydroxyurea is teratogenic in several animal studies; anomalies include nervous system, palate, skeleton, neural tube and cardiac defects. Eight case reports of hydroxyurea exposure during pregnancy did not demonstrate teratogenicity, however, the data are too limited to draw any conclusions (Briggs, 1998). Contraindicated due to high incidence of teratogenicity in animal studies and limited human experience. No longer recommended as part of HAART.

 Animal studies demonstrated amphotericin to be harmless in pregnancy. The Collaborative Perinatal Project identified 9 1st trimester exposures to amphotericin and found no adverse fetal effect (Briggs, 1998). Can be used in pregnancy for the treatment of serious fungal infections.
