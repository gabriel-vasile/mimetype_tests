ITISCode: Organizational code applied by the Integrated Taxonomic Information System (sponsored by the U.S. Department of Agriculture and allies)-each plant has a unique code. Link to http://www.itis.usda.gov/index.html for more information about coded plants.

PLANTSCode: Organizational code applied by the U.S. Department of Agriculture-each plant has a unique code. Link to http://plants.usda.gov/ for more information about coded plants.

JFamily: Plant family according to the Jepson Manual Higher Plants Of California-editor James C. Hickman-April 1993

JTrinomial: Scientific name according to the Jepson Manual Higher Plants Of California-editor James C. Hickman-April 1993

Lifeform: G = grass.  S = shrub.  T = tree.  V = vine.  F = forb (a broad-leaved herb other than a grass).

BRDCat: Categorization according to the Biological Resources Division of the U.S. Geological Survey in Sequoia National Park-the four categories are described below (excerpted from the report Exotic Species Threat Assessment ... available at the Sequoia National Park website http://www.nps.gov/seki/snrm/nnp/html/data.htm). 
Category 1 species are exotics that are currently restricted to a relatively small number of sites in each park and have either been shown to greatly affect native vegetation or have a high probability of causing serious impacts. 
Category 2 species are ones that generally have a lesser affect on native vegetation and are restricted to a relatively small number of sites. 
Category 3 species are those that have been shown to have a great effect on native vegetation: are broadly distributed in the parks: and are apparently increasing their ranges within the parks. 
Category 4 species are those that were detected by the surveys but not assigned to one of the three ranked categories and are considered low priority species. 

CALEPPCCat: Threat categorization according to the California Exotic Pest Plant Council. Link to http://groups.ucanr.org/ceppc/Pest_Plant_List/ for more information. 

CDFACat: Noxious weed designation applied by the California Department of Food and Agriculture. Link to http://www.extendinc.com/weedfreefeed/list.htm for more information.

Status: Code applied by Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks. 1 = native. 2 = naturalized alien. 3 = waif alien. 4 = extirpated native. 5 = extirpated alien. 6 = specimen identified to genus only. 7 = native to California but alien to Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks. 8 = alien immediately outside park boundary. 9 = specimen identification not verified and not in official park flora.
