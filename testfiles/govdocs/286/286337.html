David Limmer of Gas Transfer Systems (W-7) is silhouetted against a display screen as he discusses his work on molecule mobility and molecular simulation at last week's Laboratory Student Symposium. More than 50 students gave technical talks about their research this summer at Los Alamos.   enlarge image
Credit: LeRoy N. Sanchez, Public Affairs

Past, present and future

Laboratory Director Mike Anastasio talks with student Maria Castro of Subatomic Physics (P-25), her mentor, Gerd Kunde also of P-25, and Laboratory Fellow Frank Harlow at the annual Student Symposium awards banquet at University of New Mexico, Los Alamos. Anastasio thanked students and mentors for their work at Los Alamos. Harlow, a guest scientist in Theoretical (T) Division and 2004 Los Medal recipient, gave the keynote talk at the symposium awards banquet. New Students' Association officers also were named: Jake Meadows of Water Quality and RCRA (ENV-RCRA) is chairperson; Kitty Cha of the Center for Integrated Nanotechnologies (MPA-CINT) is vice-chairperson; Emily Schultz of Environmental Geology and Spatial Analysis (EES-9) is secretary; and Rachel Condi of Molecular Microbiology and Immunology (B-1) is treasurer.
