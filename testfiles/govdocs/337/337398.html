Implications of Continuous Measurement for the Uses of Census Data in Transportation Planning
Author: Bureau of Transportation Statistics
Format:  Print (Soft Cover, 108 pages)
Availability: Usually ships within 24 hours
Price: Free

Publisher: Bureau of Transportation Statistics
Publication Date: 1996
Publication Number: N/A
 
 
 

This report presents the results of a Bureau of Transportation Statistics study on the implications of this new census data-collection system are presented. Using continuous measurement, the detailed social, economic, and housing information (including journey-to-work data traditionally collected decennially with the census long-form questionnaire on a sample basis) would be obtained by an ongoing (continuous) monthly survey. The new census would obtain only population and housing unit counts and a few basic characteristics.
