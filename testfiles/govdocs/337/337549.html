FAA Releases Final Environmental Impact Statement on St. George, Utah's Proposed Replacement Municipal Airport


Renton, WA —  The Final Environmental Impact Statement (FEIS) for the proposed replacement airport of the current St. George Municipal Airport will be available to the public, on Friday, May 19, 2006, the Federal Aviation Administration announced today. The FEIS official availability will be published in the Federal Register the same date. A public comment period on the FEIS’s newly updated, refined information, and analysis on air quality and noise, to include an audibility analysis, begins that day, and closes on July 3, 2006.

The FAA prepared the FEIS to address potential environmental impacts resulting from the development of the proposed replacement airport with its 9,300-foot runway, all support facilities, passenger terminal, parking, fuel farm facilities, etc. The FEIS also presents the purpose and need for the proposed replacement airport and an analysis of possible alternatives. The document includes responses to comments received by the FAA on the Draft Environmental Impact Statement (DEIS).

The National Park Service is a cooperating federal agency for this FEIS due to potential noise impact on Zion National Park and other potentially noise-sensitive properties in the area.
