Created in 1991, the Restoration Center is the only office within NOAA solely devoted to restoring the nation’s coastal, marine, and migratory fish habitats. The Restoration Center works with a wide array of partners to restore mangrove, salt marsh, seagrass, oyster, coral reef, kelp forest, and river habitats.

The NOAA Restoration Center:

  • Funds and implements quality restoration projects to ensure healthy and sustainable fishery resources;
  • Employs technical staff to help improve project design, ensure environmental compliance, and advance restoration techniques;
  • Engages the local community and encourages stewardship of our nation’s coastal and riverine habitats;
  • Collaborates with public, private, and agency partners to prioritize projects and leverage successes; and
  • Uses scientific monitoring to evaluate restoration project success and ensure the efficient use of tax dollars.
