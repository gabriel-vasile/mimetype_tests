Section 10
Country Groupings

   This section contains information on the country and territory  
designations for use in compiling the U.S. export and import  
statistics. In addition, selected international organizations,
economic and geographic country groupings are listed.  



Country Groupings




    

  1. Country Identification: 1990

      

    1. Schedule C -- "Classification of Country and Territory 
Designations for U.S. Foreign Trade Statistics,"
contains the completeand abbreviated alphabetic 
descriptions and numeric codes for countries apprearing in
current and/or historical Bureau of Census foreign trade
data reports.

      
continent and sub-content codes (i.e., first digit of
Schedule C code) are as follows:

      
1. North America
2. Central America
3. South America
4. Europe
5. Asia
6. Australia, New Zealand and Oceania
7. Africa

    

  2. Country Table: 1989

    

      

    1. Developed and developing countries--generally follow the
country assignments made by the United Nations.
Developed countries include Australia, Canada, Japan,
New Zealand, South Africa, and Western Europe, (i.e.,
Andorra, Austria, Belgium, Cyprus, Denmark, Faroe 
Islands, Finland, France, Germany, Gibralter, Greece,
Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Liechtestein, Luxemborg, Malta,
and Goxo, Monaco, Neterlands, Norway, Portugal, San
Marino, Spain, Svalbard, Jan Mayen Island, Sweden,
Switzerland, Turkey, United Kingdon, Vatican City, and 
Yugoslavia). Developing countries include the rest of
the world except Easterm europe (i.e., Albania, Bulgaria,
Czechoslavakia, Hungary, Poland, Romania, U.S.S.R.) and
China, Mongolia, North Korea, and Vietnam.

      

    2. Developing Africa, Asia and Oceania--excludes South
Africa, Japan, Australia, and New Zealand; includes all 
other Africa, Asia and Oceania.

      

    3. Developing Western Hemisphere--excludes Canada; includes
all other North America, South America, and Central
America including island nations situated in the South
Atlantic Ocean and Caribbean Sea areas.

      

    4. APEC (Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation):
Autralia, Brunei, Canada, China, Hong Kong, Indonesia,
