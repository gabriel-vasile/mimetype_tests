Developing Leadership Skills

Program/Project Management Resource List #2

Revised Aug. 2007

"Leaders are made, they are not born. They are made by hard effort, which is the price which all of us must pay to achieve any goal that is worthwhile."-Vince Lombardi

The arts of the leader and the manager are two sides of one coin. While a manager is concerned with budgets and timetables, a leader is concerned with morale and vision. Leadership is becoming an important art at NASA as it strives to turn the words of the Vision for Space Exploration into deeds. For resources on the transition from the technical track to the managerial track, please see Transforming Engineers and Scientists Into Managers. The library also has a special bibliography on managing scientists and engineers.

All items are available at the Headquarters Library, except as noted. NASA Headquarters employees and contractors: call (358-0172) or email Library@hq.nasa.gov for information on borrowing or in-library use of any of these items.
