[The following is a response to a question posted on cc-cmt. It represents the
opinion of the NIAP Interpretations Board, a group of Common Criteria experts
assembled by CCEVS. However, it is only an opinion. Binding decisions in the
context of a specific evaluation may be provided only through the CCEVS
Observation Decision (OD) process; binding decisions in the context of all
evaluations may be provided only through the national and international
interpretations process.] 

A very strict reading of the requirement could lead one to believe that no 
change of user identity is permitted. Perhaps some rewording is in order. The 
NIB's opinion is that the requirement means that the TSF shall provide 
facilities so that the identity of the user may be determined. The TSF must 
therefore generate audit records that show who changed their identity and to 
what they changed it.
