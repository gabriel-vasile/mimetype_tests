 Summary of Safety Evaluation Areas (SEAs)
 

  • This website uses a system called SafeStat, (Safety Status), based on current federal motor carrier safety data, to measure the relative safety status of ALL Federally regulated commercial motor carriers in four Safety Evaluation Areas (SEAs): Accident, Driver, Vehicle and Safety Management.

  • SEA values range between 0 (best) and 100 (worst) and are calculated for carriers with sufficient data related to each SEA. As displayed in the bar chart, the selected company's SEA values are compared to the national averages for all Household Good Companies in each evaluation area.

  • The higher the SEA value, the worse its safety status. For example, a company with a Driver SEA value of 80 indicates that approximately 80% of the carrier population with sufficient data had a better safety performance than that company with respect to driver issues. Note: Only carriers with sufficient data are used during the calculation of the ratings.
