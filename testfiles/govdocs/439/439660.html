The principal features of the Master Plan are guidance for: 

  • Up to 14 new laboratory buildings for intramural research. The new buildings would add about 1.26 million gross square feet (gsf) of laboratory space, much of which would be utilized to decompress existing facilities.
  • A complete upgrading and modernization of support utilities and infrastructure, particularly the Central Heating and Refrigeration Plant and steam and chilled water distribution systems. 
  • Replacement of housing and care facilities for animals used in research with facilities that meet modem design, accreditation, and program requirements. 
  • Consolidation of surface parking into multiple level and underground parking structures. 
  • Construction of a Loop Road that follows existing campus streets. Emphasis is placed on pedestrian, bicycle and transit use in the central core area of the campus. 
  • A physical reorganization of the campus to improve administrative and operational functions, raise the aesthetic level or ambience, and protect older campus buildings that are potentially historic structures. 
  • Construction of a modern state-of-the-art 250 bed Clinical Center inpatient hospital with associated clinical research laboratories. The 850,000 gsf facility will replace the existing Clinical Center inpatient hospital. 
  • A complete renovation and upgrading of spaces and utilities in the remaining 2.3 million gsf Clinical Center Complex, primarily for laboratory use. Some occupiable space will be lost to upgrade and expand internal building utilities to meet modem hospital and laboratory demands. 
  • Construction of stormwater management facilities that will meet Maryland standards throughout the campus, and Montgomery County standards in the northern portion of the campus. 
  • Construction of expanded Child Day Care facilities for employees, small scale retail and service, and other employee amenities. 
  • Enhancement of a natural area or zone around the periphery of the campus through removal of surface parking and increased landscaping. The zone would buffer residential neighborhoods surrounding the campus from NIH facilities and activities. 

If the campus is developed as proposed in the Master Plan, the total occupiable building floor area would increase from seven million to ten million gross square feet by the year 2015. The current NIH campus employee population of about 16,325 is projected to fall to about 15,000 in the year 2000 due to government downsizing and budget constraint programs. Beyond 2000, a slow growth in campus employee population, most of which will occur in the last half of the planning period, is anticipated. By 2015, the number of NIH employees on campus is projected to be approximately 18,000. Increases in intramural researchers and laboratory workers will account for most of the employee increase. 

While the Master Plan makes relatively specific estimates for growth in campus population and facilities over the next 20 years, actual growth on campus will depend on future congressional and presidential policy decisions, as well as Federal budgetary constraints. Changes in national health policy could occur over the next decade, and NIH's mission could be significantly affected as a result. The Master Plan is a guiding map on how growth would take place on the Bethesda campus, were it to occur. The growth anticipated in the Master Plan may not occur to the extent indicated.
